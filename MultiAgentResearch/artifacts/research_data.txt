# 리튬 산업 전반의 감산 및 폐쇄 동향 (2023-2024)

## 시장 상황 개요

2023년부터 2024년까지 리튬 가격은 약 90%가량 하락하였으며, 이는 공급 과잉과 예상보다 낮은 전기차(EV) 수요로 인한 것입니다. 이러한 가격 하락으로 인해 많은 리튬 광산들이 생산량을 줄이거나 운영을 중단하는 상황이 발생했습니다.

## 주요 감산 및 폐쇄 사례

1. **생산량 감축 규모**: 
   - 2023년 말부터 약 190,000톤의 리튬 광산 생산능력이 감축되었으며, 약 50,000톤의 프로젝트가 지연되었습니다. (출처: CRU, Bloomberg 2024년 11월 14일 보도)
   - 호주의 리튬 광산들은 2024년 1분기에 전체적으로 약 12.3%의 생산량 감소를 기록했습니다. (출처: Antaike 통계, lithium-chemical.com)

2. **주요 기업들의 대응**:
   - **Albemarle**: 2024년 1월, 미국 최대 리튬 생산업체인 Albemarle은 인력 감축과 미국 리튬 정제소 프로젝트에 대한 지출 연기를 발표했습니다. (출처: Reuters, 2024년 1월 17일)
   - **Core Lithium**: 호주의 Core Lithium은 2024년 1월 Finniss 리튬 프로젝트의 Grants 광산 채굴을 중단했으며, 2024년 중반에는 가공 시설도 중단하고 모든 인프라를 관리 상태로 전환했습니다. (출처: Core Lithium 공식 웹사이트, Mining Technology)
   - **Pilbara Minerals**: 2024년 12월 Ngungaju 공장을 관리 상태로 전환하기로 결정했으며, 이를 통해 약 2억 호주달러(1억 3,120만 달러)의 현금 흐름 개선을 예상하고 있습니다. (출처: Fastmarkets, 2024년 10월 30일)

3. **중국 생산업체들의 동향**:
   - 여러 중국 리튬 생산업체들이 손실을 줄이기 위해 생산량 감축이나 완전한 중단을 고려하고 있습니다. (출처: Fastmarkets)
   - 2024년 9월, 중국의 한 주요 생산업체가 국내 최대 레피돌라이트(lepidolite) 광산 중 하나의 생산을 중단했으며, 이로 인해 3개의 관련 가공 공장이 폐쇄되었습니다. (출처: Fastmarkets)

## 시장 전망

1. **공급 과잉 완화 전망**:
   - 2025년에는 리튬 공급 과잉이 절반으로 줄어들 것으로 예상됩니다. (출처: Antaike, Reuters 2025년 1월 13일)
   - 중국의 전기차 보조금이 2025년 가격 지원에 도움이 될 것으로 예상됩니다. (출처: Reuters)

2. **가격 안정화 요인**:
   - 광산 폐쇄와 중국의 전기차 판매 증가로 인해 2025년에는 리튬 가격이 안정화될 것으로 분석가들은 예측하고 있습니다. (출처: Reuters)
   - 그러나 공급망에 상당한 재고가 있어 가격의 강한 회복은 예상되지 않습니다. (출처: Fastmarkets)

3. **장기 전망**:
   - 일부 분석가들은 리튬 과잉 공급이 2028년까지 지속될 수 있다고 예측하고 있습니다. (출처: Bloomberg)
   - 산업 전반의 생산 감축이나 확장 프로젝트 지연은 시장 재균형을 가속화할 것으로 예상됩니다. (출처: Discovery Alert)

# Greenbushes 리튬 광산 감산 및 운영 현황 (2023-2024)

## 기본 정보
- **프로젝트명**: Greenbushes
- **위치**: 서호주(Western Australia), 퍼스에서 남쪽으로 250km
- **운영사**: Talison Lithium (Tianqi Lithium Corporation 51%, Albemarle Corporation 49% 합작회사)
- **생산 규모**: 연간 약 1.95백만 톤의 리튬 스포듀민 생산 (세계 최대 경암 리튬 광산)
- **중요도**: 세계 리튬 스포듀민 수요의 약 1/3을 충족 가능

## 감산 관련 정보

### 2024년 감산 결정
- 2024년 1월, Greenbushes 광산은 리튬 시장 침체에 대응하여 생산량을 감축하기로 결정했습니다. (출처: ABC News, 2024년 1월 29일)
- 이는 전기차 수요 부진으로 인한 리튬 가격 하락에 따른 조치였습니다. (출처: Wikipedia)
- Talison의 대변인은 "Talison의 Greenbushes 리튬 사업은 40년 이상 리튬 산업을 선도해 왔으며 현재의 시장 변동에 대응할 수 있는 좋은 위치에 있다"고 언급했습니다. (출처: ABC News)

### 생산량 변화
- 2025 회계연도(FY2025)의 리튬 정광 생산 가이던스는 1.35-1.55백만 톤으로 설정되었습니다. (출처: Mysteel)
- 2025년 1분기 생산량은 342,000톤으로, 전분기 대비 13% 감소했으나 전년 동기 대비 22% 증가했습니다. (출처: Mysteel)
- 생산량 감소는 주로 낮은 원석 품위(feed grades)의 영향을 받았습니다. (출처: Mysteel)

### 판매 및 가격 동향
- 2025년 1분기 판매량은 366,000톤으로, 전분기 대비 17% 증가했으며 전년 동기 대비 두 배 증가했습니다. (출처: Mysteel)
- 일부 4분기 선적은 항만 혼잡으로 인해 1월로 지연되었습니다. (출처: Mysteel)
- 평균 FOB 판매 가격은 톤당 $791로 전분기 대비 7.47% 상승했습니다. (출처: Mysteel)
- 현금 비용은 톤당 AUD 341로 전분기 대비 5% 증가했으나 전년 동기 대비 12% 감소했습니다. (출처: Mysteel)

## 인력 관련 정보
- Talison의 대변인은 생산량 감축에도 불구하고 인력을 변경할 계획이 없다고 ABC에 밝혔습니다. (출처: ABC News, 2024년 1월 29일)

## 확장 계획
- 프로젝트는 일정에 따라 진행 중이며, 2025년 말까지 새로운 시설을 통해 첫 광석을 처리할 것으로 예상됩니다. (출처: Australian Mining, 2025년 7월 30일)

## 역사적 맥락
- Greenbushes의 리튬 광체는 1977년과 1980년 사이에 진행된 광범위한 시추 프로그램을 통해 확인되었으며, 광산은 30년 이상 리튬 광물을 생산하고 수출해 왔습니다. (출처: Talison Lithium 공식 웹사이트)
- 2018년 기준으로 Talison은 Greenbushes 광산을 총 용량의 60%로 운영하고 있었으며, 100% 용량으로 운영할 경우 전 세계 리튬 수요를 단독으로 충족시킬 수 있을 것으로 추정됩니다. (출처: Wikipedia)

# Core Lithium의 Finniss 프로젝트 폐쇄 및 재개 계획 (2023-2024)

## 기본 정보
- **프로젝트명**: Finniss Lithium Operation
- **위치**: 호주 노던 테리토리(Northern Territory), 다윈 항구에서 남서쪽으로 88km
- **운영사**: Core Lithium Ltd (100% 소유)
- **프로젝트 규모**: 500km² 이상의 광구 면적
- **광산 유형**: 노천 및 지하 광산

## 폐쇄 관련 정보

### 단계적 폐쇄 과정
1. **2023년 말**: 리튬 가격 하락과 비용 절감에 초점을 맞추면서 BP33(Finniss 지역 내 지하 프로젝트)의 초기 작업을 중단했습니다. 우기 채굴 조건으로 인한 복잡성도 중단 결정에 영향을 미쳤습니다. (출처: ChemAnalyst)

2. **2024년 1월**: 리튬 가격 하락으로 인해 Grants 노천 광산의 채굴을 중단했습니다. (출처: Core Lithium 공식 웹사이트, Mining Technology)

3. **2024년 중반**: 가공 시설 운영을 중단하고 모든 인프라를 관리 상태(care and maintenance)로 전환했습니다. (출처: Core Lithium 공식 웹사이트)

### 폐쇄 사유
- 리튬 가격의 급격한 하락
- 글로벌 리튬 공급 과잉
- 운영 비용 증가
- 우기 채굴 조건으로 인한 어려움
(출처: ChemAnalyst, Mining Technology)

## 재개 계획

### 재개 연구(Restart Study)
- Core Lithium은 Finniss 리튬 사업의 재개 연구가 계획대로 진행 중이며, 2025년 6월 분기에 완료될 것으로 발표했습니다. (출처: Mining Technology, ChemAnalyst)

### 재개 전략
- 재개 연구는 기존 인프라를 최적화하고 향후 채굴 및 가공 활동을 단순화하여 현장의 생산성을 향상시키는 것을 목표로 합니다. (출처: Mining Technology)
- Core는 더 낮은 운영 비용, 향상된 효율성 및 더 탄력적인 비즈니스 구조를 만들기 위해 전략적 움직임을 취하고 있습니다. (출처: Mining Technology)

### 계약 및 자산 관리
- Core는 이전 운영 계약업체로부터 "있는 그대로, 있는 곳에서(as is, where is)" 기준으로 자산을 인수할 예정입니다. (출처: Mining Technology)
- 총 A$19.5백만에 달하는 이러한 계약 문제의 해결은 2025년 7월까지 완료될 예정이며, Core의 기존 현금 준비금으로 충당될 것입니다. (출처: Mining Technology)

### 오프테이크 계약 관리
- Core Lithium은 재개 경로를 명확히 하기 위해 중국 Yahua와의 오프테이크 계약을 종료했습니다. 이는 회사가 재개 계획을 더 유연하게 추진할 수 있게 해줍니다. (출처: ChemAnalyst)

## 투자 및 개발 현황
- Core Lithium은 Finniss 프로젝트의 Grants 및 BP33 리튬 매장지에 A$2억 5천만(US$1억 5,760만) 이상을 투자했습니다. (출처: Mining Technology)
- 2021년 9월, Core Lithium의 이사회는 노던 테리토리에 위치한 완전 소유 Finniss 프로젝트 개발을 시작하기로 최종 투자 결정을 내렸습니다. (출처: Tesmanian)

## 미래 전망
- 2025년 재개 연구는 Finniss가 장기적이고 저비용의 프로젝트이며 생산 능력이 증가했음을 확인했습니다. (출처: Core Lithium 투자자 개요)
- Core Lithium은 노던 테리토리와 남호주 전역에 탐사 지역도 보유하고 있어 향후 성장 잠재력을 갖추고 있습니다. (출처: Core Lithium 투자자 개요)

# Pilbara Minerals의 Pilgangoora 프로젝트 감산 및 Ngungaju 공장 폐쇄 (2024)

## 기본 정보
- **프로젝트명**: Pilgangoora
- **위치**: 서호주(Western Australia)
- **운영사**: Pilbara Minerals
- **중요도**: 호주 최대 순수 리튬 광산 기업

## Ngungaju 공장 폐쇄 결정

### 폐쇄 시점 및 내용
- **발표 시점**: 2024년 10월 29일, Pilbara Minerals는 Ngungaju 공장을 관리 상태(care and maintenance)로 전환한다고 발표했습니다. (출처: Fastmarkets, 2024년 10월 30일)
- **폐쇄 시점**: 2024년 12월부터 Ngungaju 공장 운영 중단 (출처: Argus Media)
- **폐쇄 기간**: 2025-26 회계연도 전체 기간 동안 관리 상태 유지 계획 (출처: Argus Media)

### 폐쇄 사유
- 리튬 가격 약세로 인한 조치 (출처: Argus Media)
- 시장 공급 과잉 상황에서 생산 감축 필요성 (출처: Fastmarkets)
- "시장이 공급 과잉 상태이므로 생산 감축이 필요하며, Pilbara의 Ngungaju 공장 가동 중단 결정은 중요한 발전입니다." - William Adams, Fastmarkets 베이스 메탈 및 배터리 원자재 연구 책임자 (출처: Fastmarkets)

### 영향 및 조치
- **인력 조정**: 약 50명의 직원 감축과 나머지 50명은 다른 운영 부문으로 재배치 (출처: Australian Financial Review)
- **재정적 영향**: 이 조치로 약 A$2억(약 1억 3,120만 달러)의 현금 흐름 개선 예상 (출처: Fastmarkets)
- **생산 능력 영향**: Ngungaju 공장은 연간 180,000-200,000톤의 스포듀민 생산 능력 보유 (출처: Argus Media)

### 전략적 결정 배경
- "Ngungaju 공장은 Altura Mining으로부터 인수한 이후 상당한 업그레이드를 거쳤지만, Pilgan 공장의 규모나 가공 능력에 미치지 못합니다." - Pilbara Minerals 성명 (출처: Fastmarkets)
- 회사는 Pilgan 공장에만 집중하기로 결정 (출처: Fastmarkets)

## 전체 생산 현황 및 전망

### 2024-25 회계연도 생산 실적

# SQM의 Salar de Atacama 리튬 사업 감산 및 인력 감축 (2023-2025)

- **기업명**: SQM (Sociedad Química y Minera de Chile S.A.)
- **프로젝트**: Salar de Atacama 리튬 사업
- **위치**: 칠레 아타카마 사막
- **중요도**: 세계 2위 리튬 생산업체

## 인력 감축 및 비용 절감 조치

### 인력 감축
- **2025년 6월** SQM은 장기화된 리튬 가격 하락에 대응하기 위해 칠레 인력의 5%를 감축하기 시작했습니다. (출처: Reuters, 2025년 6월 25일)
- 이는 리튬 가격 하락과 시장 공급 과잉 상황에 대응하기 위한 조치였습니다.

### 재정 상황
- **2024년 1분기** SQM은 공급 과잉으로 인한 가격 압박과 11억 달러의 세금 판결 영향으로 8억 7천만 달러의 순손실을 기록했습니다. (출처: Reuters, 2024년 5월 23일)
- **2024년 연간 실적** 리튬 및 파생상품의 총 매출은 22억 4,130만 달러로, 2023년의 52억 달러에서 56.7% 급감했습니다. (출처: SQM 2024년 4분기 실적 발표)
- **2024년 관리비용** 총 1억 8,600만 달러(매출의 4.1%)로 집계되었습니다. (출처: SQM 2024년 4분기 실적 발표)

### 생산 전략 변화
- SQM은 2024년 1분기에 판매량을 27% 증가시켜 가격 하락을 상쇄하려 했으나, 경영진은 2분기에 더 낮은 가격을 예상했습니다. (출처: Americas Quarterly)
- 이는 가격 하락 상황에서 물량을 늘려 수익을 유지하려는 전략이었습니다.

## 사업 확장 및 미래 계획

### Codelco와의 파트너십
- SQM은 지정학적 혼란 시기에 시장 우위를 확보하기 위해 칠레 국영 구리 광산 기업인 Codelco와의 장기 파트너십에 미래를 걸고 있습니다. (출처: Americas Quarterly)
- 이 예비 공공-민간 협력은 2024년 5월에 공개 입찰이 아닌 직접 협상을 통해 체결되었으며, 2024년 11월 칠레 선거를 앞두고 점점 더 많은 조사를 받고 있습니다. (출처: Americas Quarterly)

### 생산 확대 계획
- 칠레에서 SQM은 수산화리튬 생산을 현재 35,000 메트릭 톤(MT)에서 2024년 말까지 100,000 MT로, 탄산리튬을 185,000 MT에서 2026년까지 240,000 MT로 확대할 계획입니다. (출처: Americas Quarterly)
- 2023년 기준으로 리튬 탄산염 생산 능력은 180,000 메트릭 톤이었습니다. (출처: SQM Litio 공식 웹사이트)

### Salar Futuro 프로젝트
- SQM과 Codelco 파트너십은 "염호 주변 지역사회와의 지속적인 관계를 촉진하기 위한 거버넌스 모델 구축"과 "더 효율적이고 지속 가능한 생산, 즉 더 적은 염수와 대륙 물을 사용하지 않고 더 많은 리튬을 생산하는" 새로운 추출 방법 구현을 약속하는 "Salar Futuro" 프로젝트를 추진하고 있습니다. (출처: Grist)

### 계약 연장
- SQM은 Codelco와 협약을 체결하여 2060년까지 Salar de Atacama에서의 운영을 계속할 수 있게 되었습니다. 두 회사는 운영을 위한 새로운 법인을 설립할 예정이며, Codelco가 회사의 50% 플러스 1주를 소유하게 됩니다. (출처: Investing News Network)

## 지속가능성 및 환경 영향

### 물 사용 감소 약속
- SQM은 아타카마 염호에서의 염수 추출을 2030년까지 50% 줄이기로 약속했으며, 이는 안토파가스타의 리튬 정제소 확장 계획에 영향을 미치지 않을 것이라고 밝혔습니다. (출처: SQM Litio 지속가능한 리튬 생산 보고서)
- 이러한 감축은 운영을 최적화함으로써 달성될 예정입니다.

### 환경 친화적 추출 공정

## Major Lepidolite Mine Closure
- **Status**: Production halted in September 2024
- **Details**: A major Chinese producer halted output at one of the biggest lepidolite mines in the country
- **Impact**: Caused three associated lepidolite processing plants to shut down
- **Context**: This was one of the first significant production cuts in China, occurring later than cuts in Western countries

## Chinese Production Strategy During Price Crash
- **Response to 2023 Price Crash**: While non-Chinese companies pulled back production volume significantly, Chinese-owned companies pulled back much less and in some cases continued to expand production
- **Result**: Generated an oversupply of lithium, cobalt, nickel, and natural and synthetic graphite
- **Government Support**: Chinese government policies enabled these decisions by making it financially viable for Chinese companies to sell materials at low prices that would otherwise be unsustainable

## Chinese Lithium Production Growth
- Despite market challenges, China's lithium supply grew by nearly 15% year-on-year, from 35,700 metric tons in 2023 to 41,000 metric tons in 2024
- China maintained its position as the third-largest lithium producer globally in 2024

## Inventory Levels
- By the end of 2024, Chinese lithium salt inventories stood at approximately 220,000 tonnes of LCE (Lithium Carbonate Equivalent)
- This represented about 20% of annual demand, indicating significant oversupply
- Post-Chinese Lunar New Year holiday restocking failed to meet expectations due to slow downstream buying activity and high upstream inventory levels

## Market Dynamics
- Chinese producers were not immune to weakening prices in 2024
- Higher-cost Chinese operations, particularly lepidolite mines with higher production costs, were the first to implement cuts
- The market showed relief when the major lepidolite mine halted production in September 2024
- Despite some cuts, China's overall strategy appeared to focus on maintaining market share rather than balancing supply with demand

### Inflation Reduction Act (IRA)
- **Critical Mineral Requirements**: To be eligible for a $3,750 credit, a specific percentage of the value of critical minerals (including lithium) contained in EV batteries must be extracted or processed in the United States or a country with which the United States has a free trade agreement, or be recycled in North America
- **Percentage Requirements by Year**:
  - 2023: 50%
  - 2024 and 2025: 60%
  - 2026: 70%
  - 2027: 80%
  - 2028: 90%
  - From 2029: 100%
- **Investment Impact**: The IRA provided subsidies for battery production and clean energy initiatives, generating over $224 billion in investments
- **Project Support**: In August 2019, a lithium project was initiated, but it was not until 2024 that the Department of Energy finalized a loan to support its development, including the construction of a sulfuric acid plant and a lithium processing facility

### Processing Capacity Prioritization
- **Focus Areas**: The U.S. government has prioritized the expansion of processing capacity for lithium, graphite, cobalt, and nickel
- **Market Dynamics**: Demand from domestic battery manufacturing is expected to outpace the current planned capacity for all these materials
- **Support Distribution**: Tariffs and tax incentives have resulted in favorable pricing for lithium and graphite project developers, but cobalt and nickel processing has received less support and attention

### Project Examples
- **Controlled Thermal Resources**: In January 2024, began construction of their Salton Sea lithium mine and geothermal power plant that will initially produce 25,000 metric tons of lithium, with potential for up to 175,000 metric tons once completed
- **Kings Mountain Processing Facility**: Albemarle's facility in North Carolina processes domestically extracted lithium, creating a closed domestic supply loop

## China

### Government Support for Overproduction
- **Production Strategy**: After the price crash in 2023, while other companies pulled back production significantly, Chinese-owned companies pulled back much less and in some cases continued to expand production
- **Government Role**: Chinese government policies enabled these decisions by making it financially viable for Chinese companies to sell materials at low prices that would otherwise be unsustainable
- **Market Impact**: This strategy generated an oversupply of lithium, cobalt, nickel, and natural and synthetic graphite
- **Global Reach**: Chinese companies are the primary financial backers for 80% of both the Democratic Republic of the Congo's cobalt mines and Indonesia's nickel mines
- **Lithium Acquisition**: Chinese companies have bought half of all the lithium mines offered for sale since 2018, in addition to domestically mining 18% of global lithium

### EV Subsidies
- **Subsidy Expiration**: The Chinese Government's decade-long program of subsidies for electric vehicle (EV) purchases expired in 2023
- **Market Impact**: This expiration, combined with concern of a short-term lithium oversupply and weaker-than-expected EV sales, contributed to price volatility

## Global Policy Trends

### Government Initiatives
- **Clean Energy Transition**: Government initiatives aimed at accelerating the clean energy transition are playing a significant role in boosting lithium demand
- **Policy Examples**: Policies like the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) in the U.S. and the European Critical Raw Materials Act offer financial incentives, subsidies, and tax breaks for:
  - EV production and purchases
  - Investments in raw material sourcing
  - Battery manufacturing
  - Renewable energy infrastructure

### Australia's Critical Minerals Strategy
- **Policy Framework**: The Australian Government's Critical Minerals Strategy (2023) emphasizes the importance of developing domestic resources to support both national interests and global clean energy transitions
- **Project Support**: By bringing additional lithium resources into production, projects like Core Lithium's Finniss support broader policy goals of positioning Australia as a reliable, ethical supplier of materials essential to decarbonization
- **Environmental Focus**: The strategy notes that "Critical minerals projects that demonstrate superior environmental and social practices are increasingly favored by both regulators and investors"

# Global Lithium Market Trends and Price Dynamics (2015-2024)

## Price Volatility and Crash (2022-2024)
- **Peak Prices**: Spot prices reached over $80,000 per ton in December 2022
- **Price Collapse**: By January 30, 2024, prices had fallen to just over $13,000 per ton, a decline of over 80%
- **Causes of Price Crash**: 
  - Oversupply in the market
  - Softening demand, particularly from the EV sector
  - Slower than expected rates of electric vehicle adoption
  - Decreased EV production targets in Europe and the US

## Supply and Demand Dynamics
- **Production Growth**: Global lithium production jumped from just over 737,000 tonnes in 2022 to almost 1.2 million tonnes in 2024 on a lithium carbonate equivalent (LCE) basis
- **Market Surplus**: Fastmarkets' research team estimated that the lithium market recorded a surplus of around 175,000 tonnes in 2023, and almost 154,000 tonnes in 2024
- **EV Adoption Slowdown**: The EV penetration rate in the US averaged 6.8% in the first 6 months of 2024, compared to 7.5% in 2023
- **Market Forecast**: The global lithium-ion battery market is expected to stay oversupplied until 2028 due to a decline in EV production targets in the US and EU

## Production Cuts and Project Impacts
- **Timing of Cuts**: Significant production cuts did not emerge until September 2024, when a major Chinese producer halted output at one of the biggest lepidolite mines
- **Cascading Effects**: This closure caused three associated lepidolite processing plants to shut down
- **Western Response**: Further production cut announcements emerged in the last quarter of 2024, along with project start-up delays and curtailments, mainly in Western countries
- **Australian Impact**: Higher-cost Australian projects chose to push projects into care and maintenance rather than operate at a loss
- **Specific Cuts in 2024**:
  - Finiss (Core Lithium): Operations suspended, processing stockpiles from concentrator (280kt)
  - Talison (Greenbushes): Cut of approximately 100kt
  - Kathleen Valley (Liontown): Spot price slump led to revisiting ramp-up strategy

## Price Stabilization and Future Outlook
- **Price Convergence**: Lithium prices in China and North America have converged, with North American lithium no longer enjoying the premium it did in 2023 and the first half of 2024
- **Medium-Term Forecast**: Analysts expect lithium carbonate to stay below $20 per kg globally at least through 2027
- **S&P Global Projection**: Lithium carbonate prices will stabilize near current levels in a range between $20,000/mt and $25,000/mt from 2024 to 2027
- **Price Stability Importance**: Stability is key to attracting investment, with prices needing to be low enough for battery manufacturers to profit, but high enough to encourage suppliers toward continued expansion
- **Temporary Nature**: Some analysts suggest the oversupply may be temporary due to the rapid increase in demand expected in the coming years

## Regional Dynamics
- **China's Dominance**: China has the highest production capacity for lithium-ion batteries, and its dominance is expected to continue despite trade barriers with the US and Europe
- **New Suppliers**: Lithium projects in Chile are progressing, and new suppliers like Brazil and Zimbabwe are emerging
- **US Push**: America's push for domestic production is ongoing but has not yet significantly altered the global market structure

# Comprehensive Summary: Global Lithium Project Production Cuts, Closures, and Subsidies (2015-2024)

## Market Context and Price Dynamics

The global lithium market experienced extreme volatility between 2015 and 2024, with prices reaching unprecedented highs before collapsing dramatically. After spot prices peaked at over $80,000 per ton in December 2022, they plummeted to just over $13,000 per ton by January 2024—a decline of more than 80%. This price crash was driven by significant oversupply in the market, coupled with softer-than-expected demand, particularly from the electric vehicle (EV) sector.

Global lithium production expanded rapidly, jumping from approximately 737,000 tonnes in 2022 to almost 1.2 million tonnes in 2024 on a lithium carbonate equivalent (LCE) basis. This led to substantial market surpluses—estimated at around 175,000 tonnes in 2023 and 154,000 tonnes in 2024. The oversupply situation was exacerbated by slower-than-anticipated EV adoption rates, with the EV penetration rate in the US actually declining from 7.5% in 2023 to 6.8% in the first half of 2024.

## Production Cuts and Closures by Region

### Australia

Australia, as the world's largest lithium producer, experienced the most significant production cuts in response to falling prices:

1. **Core Lithium - Finniss Project**: Operations were suspended in January 2024 due to sustained lithium price declines. The project, which was designed for 197,000 tonnes of annual spodumene production with an eight-year mine life, is now only processing stockpiles with final sales expected in Q3 2024.

2. **Pilbara Minerals - Ngungaju Plant**: Placed into care and maintenance in 2024, with approximately 50 workers shed as part of the decision to mothball the plant. The Ngungaju facility had a capacity of 200,000 tonnes.

3. **Talison Lithium - Greenbushes Mine**: Implemented a production cut of approximately 100,000 tonnes. Before the cuts, the mine had a capacity of 1.5 million tonnes per annum and was undergoing expansion to 2 million tonnes.

4. **Liontown Resources - Kathleen Valley**: Deferred expansion and ramp-up of its project as part of a cost-cutting review. The project began production in 2024 with a planned capacity of 500,000 tonnes per annum.

Overall, Australian production in Q1 2024 declined by about 12.3% compared to the previous quarter, with production cuts starting in January 2024 but building momentum throughout the year.

### China

China's response to the price crash differed significantly from Western producers:

1. **Strategic Overproduction**: While non-Chinese companies pulled back production volume significantly, Chinese-owned companies pulled back much less and in some cases continued to expand production, generating an oversupply of lithium and other battery materials.

2. **Government Support**: Chinese government policies enabled companies to sell materials at low prices that would otherwise be unsustainable, maintaining market share rather than balancing supply with demand.

3. **Lepidolite Mine Closure**: A major Chinese producer finally halted output at one of the biggest lepidolite mines in September 2024, causing three associated lepidolite processing plants to shut down. This was one of the first significant production cuts in China, occurring later than cuts in Western countries.

Despite some cuts, China's lithium supply grew by nearly 15% year-on-year, from 35,700 metric tons in 2023 to 41,000 metric tons in 2024, maintaining its position as the third-largest lithium producer globally.

### South America

South American lithium producers, particularly in Chile and Argentina, focused more on expansion than cuts during this period:

1. **SQM (Chile)**: Rather than cutting production, SQM announced plans to expand production of lithium hydroxide from 35,000 metric tons to 100,000 metric tons by the end of 2024, and to increase lithium carbonate production from 185,000 metric tons to 240,000 metric tons by 2026.

2. **Albemarle (Chile)**: Continued operations at its Atacama and La Negra facilities without significant cuts.

3. **Argentina**: Achieved an 87.5% increase in annual production in 2024, producing 33,000 tonnes of LCE in 2022, making it the second-largest producer in the region and fourth globally.

4. **Chilean Government Policy**: In April 2023, Chile announced plans for nationalizing its lithium industry, primarily affecting SQM and Albemarle. This led to new partnerships, such as the preliminary public-private deal between SQM and state-owned Codelco signed in May 2024.

### North America

North American lithium projects were largely in development rather than production during this period, so they experienced strategic shifts rather than outright cuts:

1. **Albemarle - Silver Peak (Nevada)**: The only operational lithium mine in the US maintained production but adjusted its expansion plans, aiming to increase lithium carbonate production from 5,000-10,000 tonnes per year by 2025.

2. **Albemarle - Strategic Shift**: The company deferred spending on its lithium conversion facility project in South Carolina and redirected efforts towards permitting activities for the Kings Mountain lithium-spodumene mine resource in North Carolina. It reduced 2024 capital expenditures to $1.6-1.8 billion, down from about $2.1 billion in 2023.

3. **New Projects Under Development**: Several major projects began construction, including Lithium Americas' Thacker Pass (Nevada) and Controlled Thermal Resources' Salton Sea (California) project, though both faced legal challenges.

### Africa

African lithium projects were mostly in development stages during this period, with some production adjustments:

1. **Bikita Minerals (Zimbabwe)**: Owned by Sinomine Resource Group, reduced production and cut jobs in 2024, citing poor infrastructure, inconsistent policies, and fluctuating global lithium prices.

2. **Projects in Development**: Several major projects were in development, including the Karibib Project in Namibia (expected to start production in the second half of 2025), the Goulamina Project in Mali (expected to come online in the first half of 2024), and the Ewoyaa Project in Ghana (recently granted a 15-year mining permit).

## Government Subsidies and Support

### United States

1. **Inflation Reduction Act (IRA)**: Established critical mineral requirements for EV tax credits, with increasing percentages of battery minerals (including lithium) required to be sourced from the US or free trade agreement countries—50% in 2023, rising to 100% by 2029.

2. **Department of Energy Loans**: Provided significant financial support for lithium projects, including a conditional $2.3 billion loan to Lithium Americas for its Thacker Pass project.

3. **Processing Capacity Focus**: The US government prioritized expansion of processing capacity for lithium and other battery materials, with tariffs and tax incentives resulting in favorable pricing for lithium project developers.

### China

1. **Strategic Overproduction Support**: Government policies enabled Chinese companies to maintain production despite low prices, supporting a strategy of market dominance.

2. **EV Subsidy Expiration**: The Chinese Government's decade-long program of subsidies for electric vehicle purchases expired in 2023, contributing to price volatility.

3. **Global Acquisition Strategy**: Chinese companies bought half of all lithium mines offered for sale since 2018, in addition to domestically mining 18% of global lithium, with government backing for these acquisitions.

### Australia

1. **Critical Minerals Strategy**: The Australian Government's Critical Minerals Strategy (2023) emphasized developing domestic resources to support both national interests and global clean energy transitions.

2. **Environmental Standards**: The strategy noted that "Critical minerals projects that demonstrate superior environmental and social practices are increasingly favored by both regulators and investors."

### Other Regions

1. **Chile's Nationalization**: Chile's move toward nationalizing its lithium industry represented a shift from subsidizing private companies to creating state-controlled enterprises to capture more value from lithium resources.

2. **Zimbabwe's Beneficiation Policy**: Zimbabwe announced plans to ban the export of lithium concentrates from 2027 to boost local refining and increase revenues, aiming to develop downstream industries.

## Conclusion

The period from 2015 to 2024 saw the global lithium industry transition from boom to bust and the beginnings of a potential rebalancing. Production cuts were most pronounced in high-cost operations, particularly in Australia, while Chinese producers maintained output despite falling prices. Government policies played a crucial role in shaping the industry, with the US and other Western nations focusing on securing supply chains through subsidies and domestic production incentives, while China pursued a strategy of market dominance through overproduction and strategic acquisitions.

The production cuts and project delays announced in 2023-2024, combined with continued growth in demand for lithium batteries, are expected to eventually rebalance the market. Analysts project that lithium carbonate prices will stabilize in a range between $20,000-$25,000 per metric ton from 2024 to 2027, providing a foundation for more sustainable industry growth.

## Lithium Mining Subsidies and Government Support (2015-2024)

### Australia

#### Western Australia Lithium Industry Support Program (2024)
- **Program Value**: Total value of AU$99.37 million
- **Announced**: November 2024
- **Key Components**:
  - Government Trading Enterprises (GTEs) including DevelopmentWA, Synergy, and Water Corporation will temporarily waive government fees to support the continuation of downstream processing of lithium for up to 2 years, to a total value of AU$90 million.
  - For lithium miners in the ramp-up phase of their project, port charges and mining tenement fees will be waived for up to 24 months, to the value of AU$9.37 million.
- **Rationale**: To protect critical Western Australian jobs and support the state's push to become a global battery minerals processing hub.
- **Industry Significance**: In the 2023-24 financial year, lithium miners in Western Australia generated AU$8.4 billion in sales and delivered AU$710 million in State royalties. During the same period, the industry supported more than 11,000 Western Australian jobs.

#### Australian Critical Minerals Strategies (2019-2024)
- **Total Investment**: Through 17 critical minerals strategies since 2019, Australian governments have dedicated AU$6.6 billion for critical mineral developments.
- **Global Position**: Australia led global lithium production in 2023, with five of its mines contributing more than half of the world's total output.
- **Geopolitical Context**: Australia has emerged as a critical country of interest for the United States due to its abundance of critical minerals, particularly lithium. The Biden administration has promised to extend the application of its subsidies to 'friendly' and 'like-minded' countries, including Australia.

### United States

#### Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) Provisions for Critical Minerals
- **Implementation**: The US has extended the application of its subsidies to 'friendly' and 'like-minded' countries, with Australia emerging as a critical country of interest due to its abundance of critical minerals.
- **Geopolitical Strategy**: The US is targeting China's involvement in Australian critical minerals as part of its strategy to de-risk its supply chains from China's dominance.
- **Impact on Investment**: Some analysts have argued that while the IRA is strict towards many Australia-based companies with significant Chinese ownership, investors, or processing arrangements, tapping into the subsidies and grants offered by Washington will most likely involve setting up shop in the US.

### Canada

#### North American Lithium Mine Case Study
- The North American Lithium mine near Val-d'Or, Quebec (formerly Quebec Lithium) commenced commercial production in early 2018, shipping spodumene concentrate to China for processing into lithium carbonate.
- Due to a sharp decline in lithium and spodumene concentrate prices later that year, the mine suspended operations in 2019 and sought bankruptcy protection.
- It was acquired by Sayona Quebec in 2021 in a joint venture between Sayona Mining (Australia) and Piedmont Lithium (United States).
- This case illustrates the volatility of the lithium market and the role of international investment in reviving suspended operations.

#### Critical Minerals Production
- Canada, along with Australia and the US, ranked as major critical mineral producers among the Five Eyes countries.
- From 2023 to 2030, Canada and Australia together are expected to represent 57% of planned refining projects for cobalt.

### China

#### Direct Subsidies to Battery Manufacturers
- **2022**: The Chinese government provided $401 million in subsidies to CATL (China's largest EV battery maker).
- **2023**: Subsidies to CATL increased to $809 million (more than double the 2022 amount).
- **2023**: The government provided $208.9 million to EVE Energy (China's fourth-largest EV battery producer).
- **2018-2023**: The Chinese government extended a total of $1.8 billion in subsidies to CATL alone.

#### Policy Support for Electric Vehicles and Batteries
- **2009-2017**: Buyer's rebates accounted for 62% of the Chinese government's support for the EV sector.
- **2022**: The Chinese government reduced the buyer's rebate.
- **2015-2024**: The Chinese government implemented a subsidy-centric policy that introduced fresh funds for mining, resources exploration, and processing machinery.
- **2015-2024**: Subsidies were reserved for domestic battery producers, which marked a turning point for the battery industry in China.
- **2024-2027**: China extended its tax exemption policy for New Energy Vehicles (NEVs) until 2027 to promote the electric vehicle industry.

### Global Context and Market Dynamics

#### Market Oversupply and Price Impacts
- The oversupply in the lithium market was primarily fueled by the development of spodumene mines in Australia—two in 2017 and five more in 2018.
- By the end of 2019, lithium prices had fallen to US$7,300 per tonne, and by the close of 2020, they had dropped below US$5,000 per tonne.
- This price decline led to production cuts and mine closures, particularly in Australia, as documented in the case of the North American Lithium mine in Canada.

#### Geopolitical Dimensions
- The increasingly binary geopolitical reality – China versus the US – is pushing Australia, which is rich in many critical minerals, particularly lithium, to the frontline of great power rivalry.
- This is forcing countries like Australia to apply a zero-sum geopolitical logic to their role in global supply chains, which often trumps economic rationality and green transition goals.
- Despite Chinese investment in Australia declining since 2013 and in Canada since 2019, these two countries remain among the top five destinations for Chinese State-Owned Enterprise acquisitions in the mining sectors from 2013 to 2023.

#### Processing Dominance
- China dominates lithium processing, with most of the processed products being used in battery manufacturing.

# Summary of Lithium Projects Production Cuts, Closures, and Subsidies (2015-2024)

Based on the research conducted on ten high-priority lithium projects across South America and Asia, the following key findings have emerged regarding production cuts, closures/idle periods, and subsidies between 2015 and 2024:

## 1. Production Cuts and Workforce Reductions

### South American Projects:
- **SQM (Salar de Atacama II, Chile)**: In June 2025, SQM announced a 5% reduction in its Chilean workforce (approximately 417 positions) due to stagnating lithium prices. This affected multiple business segments, with 25-30% targeting "general roles" and 70-75% affecting supervisory positions.

- **Albemarle (Salar de Atacama I, Chile)**: No specific production cuts at Salar de Atacama were identified between 2015-2024. However, in January 2024, Albemarle announced global workforce reductions and paused expansions amid falling lithium prices, and in July 2024, implemented further cost cuts due to tumbling lithium prices.

- **Argentine Projects**: In October 2024, Reuters reported that Argentina's lithium hunters were scaling back operations as the electric vehicle shift slowed. Firms cut staff, slashed spending, and halted exploration projects due to plunging lithium prices. However, no specific production cuts were reported for Olaroz, Salar del Hombre Muerto, or Cauchari-Olaroz projects.

### Asian Projects:
- **Zangge Mining's Qarhan I (Chaerhan Lake, China)**: In July 2025, this operation was ordered to halt its 10,000 tons/year lithium carbonate production capacity by local authorities due to permit issues.

- **CATL's Jianxiawo Mine (China)**: In August 2025, CATL suspended production at its mine in Yichun, Jiangxi province, after its license expired. This mine accounted for about 3-6% of global lithium output.

- **West Taijnar II (China)**: This project operated by Qinghai Hengxinrong was identified as being at risk of closure due to not having proper permits to extract lithium, though no confirmed closure was reported by 2024.

## 2. Closures and Idle Periods

No complete closures or extended idle periods were identified for the major South American projects (Salar de Atacama I & II, Olaroz, Salar del Hombre Muerto, and Cauchari-Olaroz) during the 2015-2024 period.

In China, the only confirmed closure was Zangge Mining's Qarhan I in July 2025, which was ordered to halt operations until it completed proper licensing procedures. CATL's mine closure in August 2025 was described as temporary while seeking license renewal.

## 3. Subsidies and Government Support

### South American Projects:
- **Chile**: No direct subsidies to lithium operations were identified. Instead, companies like Albemarle and SQM paid significant amounts to the Chilean government. In 2022, Albemarle paid over $600 million in taxes and royalties to the Chilean government, described as "the highest commission in the world to extract lithium."

- **Argentina**: No specific subsidies for Argentine lithium projects were identified. Argentina's approach to lithium mining has generally been to grant concessions for companies to operate rather than providing direct subsidies.

### Asian Projects:
- **China**: While specific subsidies for individual projects were not identified, China's lithium industry has benefited from government policies supporting the electric vehicle and battery sectors. In July 2024, China doubled EV subsidies, which contributed to a lithium price rally late that year. By December 2024, more than 5 million cars sold had benefited from these incentives.

## 4. Government Policy Impacts

### Chile:
- In April 2023, Chile announced plans to nationalize its lithium industry but stated it would respect existing contracts (Albemarle's runs until 2043, SQM's until 2030).
- The government is moving toward a public-private model for lithium extraction, with the state taking a controlling interest in partnerships.
- In December 2023, SQM reached a framework agreement to hand over a majority stake in its Chilean brine assets to state-owned Codelco in exchange for extending operations for three more decades.

### Argentina:
- Argentina's approach to lithium mining differs from Chile and Bolivia, with Argentina generally granting concessions for companies to operate.
- In March 2024, an Argentine court in the key lithium region halted new permits over environmental concerns, affecting the Los Patos River-Salar del Hombre Muerto area.

### China:
- By mid-2025, China had toughened its rhetoric against overcapacity, leading to production suspensions at several mines.
- China's domestic lithium supply reforms were targeting operations without proper permits, with Jiangxi's mines and certain brine producers deemed most at risk.

## 5. Market Conditions

The global lithium market experienced a dramatic price collapse, with prices falling nearly 90% from their peak in late 2022 to 2024-2025. This price decline was driven by:
- Chinese oversupply
- Weaker-than-expected demand for electric vehicles
- Excess production capacity

This market downturn led to companies worldwide scaling back investment plans, reducing workforces, and in some cases, mothballing mines. By January 2025, analysts expected to see a price recovery as production curtailments reduced the market surplus.

## Conclusion

Between 2015 and 2024, major lithium projects in South America showed remarkable resilience, with no significant production cuts or closures despite challenging market conditions. The adjustments were primarily workforce reductions and paused expansions rather than actual production cuts.

In contrast, Chinese lithium operations faced more direct interventions, with government-ordered closures for projects lacking proper permits, particularly in 2025 as part of China's domestic lithium supply reforms.

Regarding subsidies, the research found that South American projects operated without direct government subsidies, while China's lithium industry benefited from broader government support for the electric vehicle sector, particularly through consumer subsidies that indirectly supported demand for lithium products.

- 2024년 7월, 칠레 리튬이 미국 인플레이션 감축법(IRA)에 따른 세금 혜택 자격 획득

#### 아르헨티나
- 칠레, 볼리비아와 달리 기업들에게 채굴권 부여하는 방식 채택
- 직접적인 보조금 지급 사례 확인되지 않음
- 2024년 3월, 환경 문제로 주요 리튬 지역의 신규 허가 중단

#### 중국
- 2024년 7월, 전기차 보조금 두 배로 증액, 2024년 12월까지 500만 대 이상의 차량이 혜택 받음
- 2025년 중반, 과잉생산 억제 정책 강화로 여러 광산 생산 중단
- 적절한 허가 없는 운영에 대한 단속 강화, 특히 장시성 광산과 특정 염수 생산업체 위험 높음

## 시장 상황

글로벌 리튬 시장은 2022년 말 최고치 대비 2024-2025년에 약 90% 가격 하락을 경험했습니다. 이러한 가격 하락의 주요 원인:
- 중국의 과잉 공급
- 예상보다 낮은 전기차 수요
- 과잉 생산 능력

이러한 시장 침체로 전 세계 기업들이 투자 계획을 축소하고, 인력을 감축하며, 일부 광산은 가동을 중단했습니다. 2025년 1월 기준, 분석가들은 생산 감축으로 시장 과잉 공급이 줄어들면서 가격 회복을 예상했습니다.

## 결론

2015년부터 2024년까지, 남미의 주요 리튬 프로젝트들은 어려운 시장 상황에도 불구하고 놀라운 회복력을 보였으며, 중대한 생산 감축이나 폐쇄는 없었습니다. 주로 인력 감축과 확장 계획 중단 형태의 조정이 이루어졌을 뿐, 실제 생산 감축은 거의 없었습니다.

반면, 중국의 리튬 사업은 더 직접적인 개입에 직면했으며, 특히 2025년 중국의 국내 리튬 공급 개혁의 일환으로 적절한 허가가 없는 프로젝트에 대한 정부 명령 폐쇄가 있었습니다.

보조금 측면에서, 남미 프로젝트들은 직접적인 정부 보조금 없이 운영된 반면, 중국의 리튬 산업은 전기차 부문에 대한 광범위한 정부 지원, 특히 리튬 제품 수요를 간접적으로 지원하는 소비자 보조금의 혜택을 받았습니다.
