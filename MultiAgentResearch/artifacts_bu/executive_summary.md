
# North American Electric Vehicle Price Parity Analysis: Battery Cost Perspective

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines the price parity between electric vehicles (EVs) and internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles in the North American market, with a specific focus on the impact of battery costs. The study covers the period from 2020-07-31 to 2025-06-30, analyzing regional differences, vehicle segments, and key factors influencing price parity.

### Key Findings:

1. **Battery Cost Trends**: Battery pack costs have decreased from $140.00/kWh to $89.86/kWh over the analysis period, representing a 35.82% reduction.

2. **Price Parity Status**: The overall price parity index stands at 100.00/100 as of 2025-06-30, indicating that EVs are more cost-effective than comparable ICE vehicles.

3. **Regional Variations**:
   - Best region for EV adoption: California (Index: 94.61/100)
   - Worst region for EV adoption: Mexico City (Index: 49.73/100)

4. **Vehicle Segment Analysis**:
   - Most favorable segment: Compact (Index: 94.46/100)
   - Least favorable segment: Pickup (Index: 61.54/100)

5. **Optimal Market Combination**: Compact vehicles in California have the highest price parity index (100.00/100).

### Recommendations:

1. **Policy Focus**: Government incentives should be targeted toward regions with lower price parity indices to accelerate EV adoption.

2. **Industry Strategy**: Manufacturers should prioritize battery cost reduction and focus on segments with higher price parity potential.

3. **Consumer Education**: Highlight total cost of ownership benefits in regions where upfront price parity has not yet been achieved.

4. **Infrastructure Investment**: Concentrate charging infrastructure development in regions approaching price parity to remove adoption barriers.

5. **Research Direction**: Continue research on battery chemistry innovations that can further reduce costs below the critical $100/kWh threshold.
