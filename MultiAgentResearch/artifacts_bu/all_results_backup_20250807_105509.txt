Price Parity Year Projections by Scenario:

Scenario Parameters:
- Pessimistic: 5% annual battery cost reduction, no incentives, 2% manufacturing efficiency improvement, 3% annual raw material cost increase
- Base Case: 8% annual battery cost reduction, $7,500 incentives, 3% manufacturing efficiency improvement, stable raw material costs
- Optimistic: 12% annual battery cost reduction, $10,000 incentives, 5% manufacturing efficiency improvement, 3% annual raw material cost decrease
- Breakthrough: 15% annual battery cost reduction, $12,500 incentives, 7% manufacturing efficiency improvement, 5% annual raw material cost decrease

Key Sensitivity Factors (Impact on Price Parity Year):
1. Battery Cost Reduction Rate: Most significant factor - higher reduction rates accelerate price parity by 2-5 years
2. Government Incentives: Direct impact - each $2,500 in incentives can accelerate price parity by approximately 1 year
3. Manufacturing Efficiency: Moderate impact - improvements help offset raw material cost fluctuations
4. Raw Material Costs: Significant impact - volatility can delay or accelerate price parity by 1-3 years

Key observations:
- Under the Base Case scenario, price parity is projected to be reached by 2033
- The Breakthrough scenario could bring price parity forward to 2026 through a combination of technological advances and policy support
- Battery cost reduction remains the most critical factor for achieving price parity
- Government incentives can significantly accelerate price parity, especially in the near term
- Raw material cost volatility presents a significant risk to price parity projections
