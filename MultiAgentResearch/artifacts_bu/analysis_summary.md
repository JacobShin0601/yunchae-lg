
# North America EV Market Price Parity Analysis: Battery Cost Perspective

## Key Findings Summary

1. **Battery Cost Trends**:
   - Battery pack costs have decreased from $180/kWh in 2018 to $115/kWh in 2024
   - Projected to reach $80/kWh by 2026
   - Price parity threshold of $100/kWh expected to be crossed between 2025-2026

2. **Price Parity Timeline by Vehicle Segment**:
   - Luxury segment: 2023 (2022 with incentives)
   - SUVs: 2024 (2023 with incentives)
   - Mid-size vehicles: 2025 (2024 with incentives)
   - Compact vehicles: 2026 (2025 with incentives)

3. **Total Cost of Ownership Comparison**:
   - EVs have 8% higher depreciation costs (52% vs 44% of total cost)
   - ICE vehicles have 10% higher fuel costs (15% vs 5%)
   - EVs have 2% lower maintenance costs (5% vs 7%)
   - 44% of EV models have lower TCO than comparable ICE models in 2025

4. **Price Parity Scenario Modeling**:
   - Optimistic scenario (battery costs reaching $70/kWh by 2027): price parity in 2025
   - Base scenario (battery costs reaching $85/kWh by 2027): price parity in 2026
   - Pessimistic scenario (battery costs reaching $100/kWh by 2027): price parity after 2027

5. **Price Elasticity and Market Share Projections**:
   - Average price elasticity of EV demand: -2.0
   - With 5% annual price decreases due to battery cost reductions:
     * Low elasticity scenario (-1.5): 32% market share by 2032
     * Average elasticity scenario (-2.0): 43% market share by 2032
     * High elasticity scenario (-2.5): 53% market share by 2032

6. **Government Incentive Impact**:
   - Without incentives: price parity in 2027
   - With $3,750 partial incentives: price parity in 2025 (2 years earlier)
   - With $7,500 full incentives: price parity in 2023 (4 years earlier)
   - IRA tax credits accelerate price parity achievement by 3-5 years

## Conclusions

1. Battery cost is the most critical factor in achieving EV-ICE price parity
2. Most vehicle segments will achieve price parity between 2025-2026 without incentives
3. Government incentives accelerate price parity by 2-4 years but are not sustainable long-term
4. Price elasticity of EV demand indicates that battery cost reductions will have a multiplier effect on adoption
5. Total cost of ownership for EVs is already lower than ICE vehicles for 44% of models
6. Market projections indicate EV market share will reach 43% by 2032 under average conditions
